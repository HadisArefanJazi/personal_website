Bio

My research studies how a decision maker with limited attention allocates attention over time across noisy information sources to make accurate decisions, using rational inattention framework. I focus on conditions that make the optimal attention strategy robust and time-consistent, so the decision maker does not regret the strategy even if the performance measure changes over time. This is motivated by modern decision environments where AI produces large, fast-changing streams of information and effective outcomes depend on how limited human attention is distrbuted.
